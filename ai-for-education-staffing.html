Why Web Forms Fail Substitute and Paraprofessional Candidates

Web forms were designed for office workers sitting at desks with reliable Wi-Fi and 20 uninterrupted minutes. Substitute teachers, paraprofessionals, and instructional aides are not that candidate. They move between buildings, work odd hours, and live on their phones — and they are not going to open a 15-field online application between assignments.

The data makes this concrete:

  • Mobile form completion rates in staffing hover around 20%. Four out of five candidates who tap "Apply" on a job board drop out before finishing. That is not a candidate motivation problem — it is a tool problem.
  • Every additional field on a form reduces completion by roughly 10%. A credentialing-heavy education intake packet covering certifications, background check eligibility, grade-level preferences, and district-specific restrictions is mathematically doomed on mobile before a candidate even reaches the middle of the form.
  • Chatbot-style intake tools have the same fundamental flaw: they still require a candidate to stare at a screen and type. That is the same friction point dressed in different packaging. The candidate's thumbs still have to do the work, and most candidates stop before they finish.
  • When a candidate ghosts a form, the incomplete record lands in your ATS and triggers a recruiter follow-up call. Each of those follow-up cycles costs approximately 25 minutes of recruiter time — time that should be spent on placement, not data collection.

The compounding effect is significant. If your agency processes 40 applications per week and 80% drop off, your recruiters are spending hours each week attempting to recover data that should have been captured the first time. Meanwhile, a competing agency that converts those same candidates on a voice call is building a deeper, faster-moving pipeline from the same applicant pool.

The form is not the candidates' problem to solve. It is yours — and Axiom solves it.
