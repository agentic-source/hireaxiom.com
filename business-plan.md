## 1. Executive Summary

### The Concept

We sell pre-configured Mac Mini workstations loaded with AI automation software (Claude Code, custom agents, and industry-specific workflows) as an always-on AI assistant for small and mid-size businesses. Each unit ships with white-glove setup, a business-context file tuned to the customer's operations, and an ongoing monthly support retainer.

This is not a chatbot. This is a dedicated AI team member that runs 24/7 on the customer's desk or network -- scraping job boards, drafting contracts, analyzing data, managing email workflows, generating reports, and executing multi-step business processes autonomously.

## 2. Problem Statement

### The AI Implementation Gap

Every small business owner has heard about AI. Most have tried ChatGPT or Microsoft Copilot. Almost none have figured out how to make AI do real, repeatable work in their business. The gap between "AI is amazing" and "AI runs part of my business" is enormous, and almost nobody is bridging it.

### Specific Pain Points

**1. Generic AI tools do not do custom work.**
ChatGPT can write an email. It cannot scrape a job board every morning at 6 AM, cross-reference results against your open requirements, draft personalized outreach to matching candidates, and log everything in a spreadsheet. Cloud AI tools are conversational -- they answer questions when asked. They do not execute multi-step business processes autonomously.

**2. Hiring AI talent is prohibitively expensive.**
An AI consultant charges $150-250/hour. A full-time AI engineer costs $150,000-250,000/year. For a 10-person staffing agency or a solo law practice, these numbers are non-starters. Even if they could afford it, there is no guarantee the consultant understands their specific industry.

**3. There is no always-on, dedicated AI infrastructure for SMBs.**
Cloud tools require a human to open a browser, type a prompt, and wait for a response. There is no device that sits on a business owner's desk and continuously works -- monitoring, scraping, analyzing, generating, and reporting -- without being prompted.

**4. Security and data control matter.**
Many business owners are uncomfortable pasting client data, contracts, or financial information into cloud-based AI tools. A local device that processes sensitive data on-premises (with cloud API calls only when needed) addresses a real objection.

**5. Context is everything, and cloud tools have none.**
Every time a business owner opens ChatGPT, they start from zero. There is no persistent memory of their business, their clients, their preferences, their industry terminology, or their workflows. They spend half their time re-explaining their business before they can get useful output.

## 3. Solution

### The Axiom AI Workstation

A Mac Mini M4 (or M4 Pro) pre-configured with:

- **Claude Code** -- Anthropic's CLI agent for code generation, automation, and agentic workflows
- **Custom automation agents** -- Tailored to the customer's industry and specific business processes
- **CLAUDE.md business context file** -- A comprehensive document pre-loaded with the customer's business information, industry knowledge, terminology, preferences, client details, and operational procedures. This gives every AI interaction full context from the first prompt.
- **Scheduled tasks** -- Cron jobs and background agents that run autonomously (daily scraping, weekly reports, automated monitoring)
- **Development environment** -- Node.js, Python, shell tools, and any libraries needed for the customer's automations
- **Remote access** -- SSH and screen sharing configured for remote support and maintenance

### How It Works

1. **Discovery call** (60-90 minutes) -- We learn the customer's business, workflows, pain points, and automation opportunities.
2. **Configuration** (1-2 weeks) -- We build their CLAUDE.md, create custom automations, test everything, and document the setup.
3. **Deployment** -- We ship the configured Mac Mini (or deploy on-site for Enterprise tier). We do a 90-minute walkthrough with the customer.
4. **Ongoing support** -- Monthly retainer covers maintenance, updates, new automations, troubleshooting, and optimization.

### What the Customer Experiences

They walk into their office in the morning and their AI assistant has already:

- Scraped job boards and flagged 12 new positions matching their open requirements
- Drafted outreach emails for the top 5 candidates
- Generated a weekly revenue report from their billing data
- Flagged a contract clause that deviates from their standard terms
- Sent a follow-up email to 3 clients who have not responded in 5 business days

This is not hypothetical. Tyler Meeks does this today, every day, with a Mac and Claude Code.

### Comparison Matrix

| Feature | Axiom | Microsoft Copilot | ChatGPT Enterprise | AI Consultant | Human VA |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Always-on (24/7) | Yes | No | No | No | No |
| Custom automations | Yes | No | Limited | Yes (project-based) | No |

| Local data processing | Yes | No | No | Varies | N/A |
| Web scraping | Yes | No | No | Yes | Manual |

| Business context memory | Yes (CLAUDE.md) | Limited | Limited | Project docs | Training |
| Monthly cost | $499-1,999 | $30/user | $60/user | $150-250/hr | $2,000-6,000 |
| Setup effort | Plug and play | Self-serve | Self-serve | Weeks-months | Weeks |
| Ongoing support | Included | Self-serve | Self-serve | Additional cost | Management overhead |

### Competitive Advantages

1. **Always-on execution.** Cloud AI tools are reactive -- they respond when prompted. Axiom runs proactively: scraping, monitoring, generating, and reporting without human initiation.

2. **Deep business context.** The CLAUDE.md file gives every interaction full knowledge of the customer's business. No other product offers this level of persistent, customizable context.

3. **Local + cloud hybrid.** Sensitive data stays on the local device. API calls to Claude handle the intelligence. This addresses security concerns that keep many SMBs away from cloud-only AI tools.

4. **Vertical expertise.** We do not try to be everything to everyone. We build deep automation suites for specific industries, starting with healthcare staffing where we have first-hand operational experience.

5. **Tangible product.** A physical device on their desk is psychologically different from another SaaS subscription. It feels like hiring someone. It is always there, always working.

6. **Ongoing relationship.** The monthly retainer creates a long-term partnership. We optimize, expand, and adapt the system as the customer's business evolves. AI consultants finish a project and leave.

### Configuration & Testing Process

**Standard configuration checklist (8-15 hours per unit):**

1. macOS setup, security hardening, remote access configuration
2. Install development tools: Homebrew, Node.js, Python, Git, Pandoc
3. Install and authenticate Claude Code
4. Create CLAUDE.md from discovery call notes
5. Build custom automations (varies by tier)
6. Configure scheduled tasks (cron, launchd)
7. Test all automations end-to-end
8. Document the setup for the customer and for internal support reference
9. Package and ship (or deploy remotely)

**Quality gate:** Every unit must pass a 24-hour burn-in test with all automations running before shipping.

## 11. Risks & Mitigations

### Risk 1: API Cost Volatility

**Risk:** Anthropic changes Claude API pricing, significantly increasing per-unit costs.
**Likelihood:** Medium. API prices have generally trended downward, but pricing models could change.
**Impact:** High. API costs are a major component of COGS.
**Mitigation:**
- Build a 30% buffer into API cost assumptions in pricing.
- Use prompt caching (90% input cost reduction) and batch API (50% discount) aggressively.
- Monitor per-customer API usage monthly. Adjust retainer pricing annually if needed.
- Maintain the ability to swap AI providers (build automations to be somewhat provider-agnostic where feasible).

### Risk 2: Apple Hardware Changes

**Risk:** Apple discontinues the Mac Mini, significantly changes pricing, or shifts to a form factor that does not work for this use case.
**Likelihood:** Low. The Mac Mini has been in Apple's lineup for 20 years. The M4 generation moved to a smaller form factor that is ideal for desk deployment.
**Impact:** Medium. Would require product redesign but not a fundamental business model change.
**Mitigation:**
- Maintain relationships with Apple Business for early notice of product changes.
- The business model works with any always-on Mac (Mac Studio, iMac, even a MacBook in clamshell mode).
- Could adapt to other hardware platforms (Linux mini PCs) if necessary, though macOS is a strong differentiator.

### Risk 3: Claude Code Deprecation or Major Changes

**Risk:** Anthropic discontinues Claude Code, makes it enterprise-only, or fundamentally changes its capabilities.
**Likelihood:** Low-Medium. Claude Code is a flagship Anthropic product with growing adoption.
**Impact:** Very High. Claude Code is the core technology.
**Mitigation:**
- Build relationships with Anthropic (potential partnership, early access programs).
- Abstract automation logic so it is not 100% dependent on Claude Code specifically.
- Monitor alternative agentic AI tools (Cursor, Aider, Cline) as fallback options.
- Consider becoming an Anthropic partner or reseller for preferred access and pricing.

### Risk 4: Support Burden Scaling

**Risk:** As the customer base grows, support requests overwhelm the team. Quality drops, churn increases.
**Likelihood:** High. This is the most likely operational challenge.
**Impact:** High. Poor support directly drives churn.
**Mitigation:**
- Build a robust knowledge base and runbooks from Day 1.
- Standardize configurations within each vertical to reduce unique support scenarios.
- Implement remote monitoring and proactive maintenance to catch issues before customers report them.
- Hire support staff ahead of the curve (hire at 70% capacity, not 100%).
- Tiered support model ensures the highest-paying customers get the best service.

### Risk 5: Competition from Big Tech

**Risk:** Microsoft, Google, or Apple launches a similar product -- a pre-configured AI workstation for SMBs.
**Likelihood:** Medium-Long term. Big tech is focused on cloud SaaS, not hardware+service bundles.
**Impact:** Medium. Their products would likely be generic, not vertical-specific.
**Mitigation:**
- Move fast and build deep vertical expertise that big tech will not replicate.
- Customer relationships and switching costs (custom automations, CLAUDE.md context) create stickiness.
- Big tech entry validates the market and expands awareness.
- Focus on service quality and customization that large companies cannot match.

### Risk 6: Customer Churn

**Risk:** Customers cancel after a few months because they do not see enough value or the AI does not meet expectations.
**Likelihood:** Medium. Some customers will have unrealistic expectations.
**Impact:** High. Recurring revenue is the core of the business model.
**Mitigation:**
- Set clear expectations during sales process. Show exactly what the system can and cannot do.
- 60-day money-back guarantee reduces buyer hesitation and forces us to deliver value fast.
- Monthly ROI reporting: show customers exactly how much time and money their Axiom has saved.
- Quarterly optimization sessions (Enterprise) to ensure the system evolves with the business.
- Target 90%+ annual retention rate.

### Risk 7: Regulatory and Compliance

**Risk:** Industry-specific regulations (HIPAA in healthcare, data privacy laws) create liability.
**Likelihood:** Medium. Healthcare staffing involves protected health information.
**Impact:** Medium-High.
**Mitigation:**
- Do not store PHI on the Axiom unless the customer has a BAA in place.
- Offer HIPAA-compliant configuration as an add-on for healthcare customers.
- Consult with a healthcare IT compliance attorney before serving medical practices.
- Include clear terms of service and liability limitations in customer agreements.
